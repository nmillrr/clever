## Extension Name
Academic Access Extension

## Summary (132 characters max)
Access paywalled academic articles through your university library subscriptions. Detects paywalls and redirects via institutional proxies.

## Detailed Description

**Academic Access Extension** helps students, researchers, and academics easily access paywalled articles through their institutional subscriptions. The extension automatically detects when you've encountered a paywall and provides one-click access via your university's proxy server.

### Key Features

**🔍 Automatic Paywall Detection**  
The extension identifies paywalled content across major academic publishers including JSTOR, Elsevier, Springer, Wiley, Taylor & Francis, Oxford University Press, SAGE, IEEE, ACM, Nature, and more.

**🏛️ Institutional Access**  
Seamlessly redirects to your university's proxy server for accessing subscribed content. Works with various proxy systems (EZProxy, prefix URLs, domain suffixes).

**🔄 Multiple Institution Support**  
Configure multiple institutional affiliations and easily switch between them—perfect for researchers with multiple university connections.

**📄 Metadata Extraction**  
Automatically extracts DOIs, PMIDs, arXiv IDs, and other academic identifiers to ensure accurate redirects.

**📚 Open Access Alternatives**  
When institutional access isn't available, the extension searches for free, legal alternatives via Unpaywall.

**📋 Access History**  
Keeps track of your accessed articles for easy reference.

**🔒 Privacy-Focused**  
All your data stays on your device. No personal information is collected or transmitted.

### How It Works

1. **Set Up**: Select your institution from our list or enter custom proxy details.
2. **Browse**: Visit any academic article or publication website.
3. **Access**: When a paywall is detected, click the "Access via Institution" button.
4. **Research**: You'll be redirected through your institutional proxy to the full text.

### Privacy & Security

The extension only stores your institution settings and article history locally on your device. No personal data is collected or transmitted to external servers. All extension code is open source and available for review.

### Who Should Use This Extension?

- University students needing access to course readings
- Academic researchers navigating multiple journal subscriptions
- Librarians helping patrons with electronic resources
- Anyone affiliated with an institution that provides proxy access to academic resources
